TEMA 1: ADJETIVOS (ADJECTIVES)
================================================================================

UNIT TITLE:
Adjectives

PARALLEL:
1ro BGU B - 2025-2026

BRIEF SUMMARY:
Learn to describe people, places, and things using descriptive words. Master the different types of adjectives and their correct position in sentences.

THEORY CONTENT (Visual Design):

[TITLE]
What are Adjectives?

[PARAGRAPH]
Adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns (people, places, things, or ideas). They give us more information about the qualities or characteristics of something.

[RULE]
Position of Adjectives:
• Before the noun: a beautiful house
• After the verb "to be": The house is beautiful

[TITLE]
Types of Adjectives

[PARAGRAPH]
1. Descriptive: beautiful, tall, small, happy, sad
2. Quantity: many, few, several, some
3. Demonstrative: this, that, these, those
4. Possessive: my, your, his, her, its, our, their

[TITLE]
Common Adjectives

[PARAGRAPH]
Size: big, small, large, tiny, huge
Age: old, new, young, ancient, modern
Temperature: hot, cold, warm, cool, freezing
Speed: fast, slow, quick, rapid
Feelings: happy, sad, angry, excited, tired
Appearance: beautiful, ugly, pretty, handsome
Quality: good, bad, excellent, terrible, perfect

[TITLE]
Examples

[PARAGRAPH]
• She has a red car. (Ella tiene un carro rojo)
• The tall man is my father. (El hombre alto es mi padre)
• This is an interesting book. (Este es un libro interesante)
• I have two big dogs. (Tengo dos perros grandes)
• The weather is cold today. (El clima está frío hoy)

TEMA 2: ADJETIVOS COMPARATIVOS (COMPARATIVE ADJECTIVES)
================================================================================

UNIT TITLE:
Comparative Adjectives

PARALLEL:
1ro BGU B - 2025-2026

BRIEF SUMMARY:
Compare two people, places, or things using comparative forms. Learn the rules for short, long, and irregular adjectives.

THEORY CONTENT (Visual Design):

[TITLE]
What are Comparative Adjectives?

[PARAGRAPH]
Comparative adjectives are used to compare two people, places, or things. They show the difference in quality, quantity, or degree between them.

[RULE]
Formation Rules:

1. SHORT ADJECTIVES (1 syllable):
   Add -er + than
   • tall → taller than
   • old → older than
   • fast → faster than

2. ADJECTIVES ENDING IN -Y:
   Change -y to -ier + than
   • happy → happier than
   • easy → easier than
   • pretty → prettier than

3. LONG ADJECTIVES (2+ syllables):
   Use more + adjective + than
   • beautiful → more beautiful than
   • expensive → more expensive than
   • interesting → more interesting than

4. IRREGULAR ADJECTIVES:
   • good → better than
   • bad → worse than
   • far → farther/further than

[TITLE]
Common Comparatives

[PARAGRAPH]
Short adjectives:
• big → bigger than
• small → smaller than
• hot → hotter than
• cold → colder than
• young → younger than
• cheap → cheaper than

Long adjectives:
• difficult → more difficult than
• comfortable → more comfortable than
• important → more important than
• dangerous → more dangerous than

[TITLE]
Examples

[PARAGRAPH]
• John is taller than Mary. (John es más alto que Mary)
• This book is more interesting than that one. (Este libro es más interesante que ese)
• My car is faster than yours. (Mi carro es más rápido que el tuyo)
• She is happier than before. (Ella está más feliz que antes)
• Today is better than yesterday. (Hoy es mejor que ayer)

TEMA 3: ADJETIVOS SUPERLATIVOS (SUPERLATIVE ADJECTIVES)
================================================================================

UNIT TITLE:
Superlative Adjectives

PARALLEL:
1ro BGU B - 2025-2026

BRIEF SUMMARY:
Express the highest degree of a quality among three or more items. Master the superlative forms for different types of adjectives.

THEORY CONTENT (Visual Design):

[TITLE]
What are Superlative Adjectives?

[PARAGRAPH]
Superlative adjectives are used to show the highest or lowest degree of a quality among three or more people, places, or things. They indicate that something is at the extreme end of a scale.

[RULE]
Formation Rules:

1. SHORT ADJECTIVES (1 syllable):
   the + adjective + -est
   • tall → the tallest
   • old → the oldest
   • fast → the fastest

2. ADJECTIVES ENDING IN -Y:
   the + adjective (change -y to -iest)
   • happy → the happiest
   • easy → the easiest
   • pretty → the prettiest

3. LONG ADJECTIVES (2+ syllables):
   the most + adjective
   • beautiful → the most beautiful
   • expensive → the most expensive
   • interesting → the most interesting

4. IRREGULAR ADJECTIVES:
   • good → the best
   • bad → the worst
   • far → the farthest/furthest

[TITLE]
Common Superlatives

[PARAGRAPH]
Short adjectives:
• big → the biggest
• small → the smallest
• hot → the hottest
• cold → the coldest
• young → the youngest

Long adjectives:
• difficult → the most difficult
• comfortable → the most comfortable
• important → the most important
• beautiful → the most beautiful

[TITLE]
Examples

[PARAGRAPH]
• Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world. (El Monte Everest es la montaña más alta del mundo)
• She is the smartest student in the class. (Ella es la estudiante más inteligente de la clase)
• This is the most expensive car in the store. (Este es el carro más caro de la tienda)
• He is the best player on the team. (Él es el mejor jugador del equipo)
• That was the worst movie I've ever seen. (Esa fue la peor película que he visto)

TEMA 4: PREPOSICIONES DE LUGAR (PREPOSITIONS OF PLACE)
================================================================================

UNIT TITLE:
Prepositions of Place

PARALLEL:
1ro BGU B - 2025-2026

BRIEF SUMMARY:
Learn to describe the location and position of objects and people using prepositions like in, on, at, under, behind, and more.

THEORY CONTENT (Visual Design):

[TITLE]
What are Prepositions of Place?

[PARAGRAPH]
Prepositions of place are words that show the position or location of people, objects, or places in relation to other things.

[RULE]
Main Prepositions:

IN - inside or within an enclosed space
• The book is in the bag.
• She lives in New York.

ON - on a surface
• The book is on the table.
• The picture is on the wall.

AT - at a specific point or location
• I'm at the bus stop.
• She is at school.

UNDER - below or beneath something
• The cat is under the table.
• The shoes are under the bed.

BEHIND - at the back of something
• The car is behind the house.
• He is hiding behind the door.

IN FRONT OF - facing something
• The car is in front of the house.
• She is standing in front of me.

[TITLE]
More Prepositions

[PARAGRAPH]
NEXT TO / BESIDE - at the side of
• The bank is next to the supermarket.

BETWEEN - in the space separating two things
• The book is between the lamp and the clock.

ABOVE - higher than something
• The plane is above the clouds.

BELOW - lower than something
• The temperature is below zero.

NEAR - close to something
• The school is near my house.

[TITLE]
Examples

[PARAGRAPH]
• The cat is sleeping on the sofa. (El gato está durmiendo en el sofá)
• There is a park near my house. (Hay un parque cerca de mi casa)
• The restaurant is between the bank and the pharmacy. (El restaurante está entre el banco y la farmacia)
• Put the book on the shelf. (Pon el libro en el estante)
• The dog is under the tree. (El perro está debajo del árbol)

TEMA 5: THERE IS / THERE ARE
================================================================================

UNIT TITLE:
There is / There are

PARALLEL:
1ro BGU B - 2025-2026

BRIEF SUMMARY:
Express the existence of things using "there is" for singular and "there are" for plural. Learn affirmative, negative, and question forms.

THEORY CONTENT (Visual Design):

[TITLE]
What is "There is / There are"?

[PARAGRAPH]
We use "there is" and "there are" to say that something exists or to indicate the presence of people or things.

• THERE IS = singular (one thing)
• THERE ARE = plural (more than one thing)

[RULE]
AFFIRMATIVE FORMS:

There is + singular noun
• There is a book on the table.
• There is a cat in the garden.
• There is a teacher in the classroom.

There are + plural noun
• There are two books on the table.
• There are many students in the school.
• There are three apples in the basket.

[RULE]
NEGATIVE FORMS:

There is not (isn't) + singular noun
• There is not a book on the table.
• There isn't a cat in the garden.
• There isn't any milk in the fridge.

There are not (aren't) + plural noun
• There are not two books on the table.
• There aren't many students in the school.
• There aren't any apples in the basket.

[RULE]
QUESTION FORMS:

Is there + singular noun + ?
• Is there a book on the table?
  → Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.
• Is there any milk in the fridge?
  → Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.

Are there + plural noun + ?
• Are there books on the table?
  → Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.
• Are there many students in the school?
  → Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.

[TITLE]
Questions with "How many"

[PARAGRAPH]
• How many books are there on the table?
  → There are three books.
• How many students are there in the class?
  → There are twenty students.

[TITLE]
Examples

[PARAGRAPH]
Affirmative:
• There is a dog in the park. (Hay un perro en el parque)
• There are three cars in the garage. (Hay tres carros en el garaje)

Negative:
• There isn't a problem. (No hay un problema)
• There aren't any cookies. (No hay galletas)

Questions:
• Is there a supermarket near here? (¿Hay un supermercado cerca de aquí?)
• Are there any questions? (¿Hay alguna pregunta?)

TEMA 6: PLACES OF THE CITY (LUGARES DE LA CIUDAD)
================================================================================

UNIT TITLE:
Places of the City

PARALLEL:
1ro BGU B - 2025-2026

BRIEF SUMMARY:
Learn vocabulary for common places in a city and how to give and understand directions. Essential for everyday conversations and navigation.

THEORY CONTENT (Visual Design):

[TITLE]
Common Places in the City

[PARAGRAPH]
Learning the names of places in a city helps you navigate, give directions, and have everyday conversations.

[TITLE]
Essential Places

[PARAGRAPH]
PUBLIC SERVICES:
• Bank - banco
• Hospital - hospital
• Police Station - estación de policía
• Fire Station - estación de bomberos
• Post Office - oficina de correos
• Library - biblioteca

EDUCATION & CULTURE:
• School - escuela
• Museum - museo
• Church - iglesia
• Cinema / Movie Theater - cine

SHOPPING:
• Supermarket - supermercado
• Shopping Mall - centro comercial
• Pharmacy / Drugstore - farmacia
• Bakery - panadería

FOOD & DRINK:
• Restaurant - restaurante
• Cafe / Coffee Shop - cafetería

[TITLE]
More Places

[PARAGRAPH]
TRANSPORTATION:
• Airport - aeropuerto
• Train Station - estación de tren
• Bus Stop - parada de autobús
• Gas Station - gasolinera

RECREATION:
• Park - parque
• Gym - gimnasio
• Stadium - estadio
• Zoo - zoológico
• Beach - playa

ACCOMMODATION:
• Hotel - hotel

CITY FEATURES:
• Street - calle
• Avenue - avenida
• Bridge - puente
• Square / Plaza - plaza

[TITLE]
Useful Phrases

[PARAGRAPH]
Asking for directions:
• Where is the bank? (¿Dónde está el banco?)
• How do I get to the hospital? (¿Cómo llego al hospital?)
• Is there a supermarket near here? (¿Hay un supermercado cerca de aquí?)

Giving directions:
• Go straight. (Ve derecho)
• Turn left/right. (Gira a la izquierda/derecha)
• It's on the corner. (Está en la esquina)
• It's next to the park. (Está al lado del parque)
• It's across from the bank. (Está frente al banco)

[TITLE]
Examples

[PARAGRAPH]
• The supermarket is next to the pharmacy. (El supermercado está al lado de la farmacia)
• There is a bank on Main Street. (Hay un banco en la calle principal)
• The hospital is near the school. (El hospital está cerca de la escuela)
• I need to go to the post office. (Necesito ir a la oficina de correos)
• The museum is across from the park. (El museo está frente al parque)
